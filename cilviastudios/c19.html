C19 Temperature Gun

© MUSTAFA AL QURAISHI, CILVIA STUDIOS 2020

Although not common in the UK, temperature guns are being used in areas such as China to monitor potential virus carriers in public. The issue is is that they are notoriously inaccurate, due to the fact that the environment ie. dust and holding the gun away from the subject at an incorrect distance leads to faulty readings. This designs mitigates the issue of incorrect distance readings, and thus provides consistent accurate readings from long and short range readings. This is achieved by the use of ultrasonic driver and a dielectric lens to measure the correct distance of the object to the camera, using this information to change the refraction index of the lens and thus focus the lasers to a single point, ensuring the operator that they are operating at a correct distance and thus the main body thermal sensor camera can take an accurate reading. The back main screen will display a red/green feedback relative to a temperature too high or low set by the operator, set by the rotating dial. Enabling quick reading of public and thus reducing the occurrence of potential virus carriers moving around in public. The design features a USB-C port for charging and data extraction, a single press capture button and a on/off swivel dial on the lateral side, this dial can be designed to enable operation in various modes. Although it was an aim to avoid the form of a gun shape, it was found to be the most easy to handle for constant operation, the features are minimal and dialled to provide a sleek, tech product.
